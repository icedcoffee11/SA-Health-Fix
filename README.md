## Executive Memorandum

**TO:** Premier, Cabinet, Treasurer  
**FROM:** Scott Muller  
**SUBJECT:** Securing SA Health Capacity – 12-Month Flow Redesign with \$150M–\$400M Net ROI  

### 1. The Problem We Must End

- South Australia is currently experiencing **5,550+ ramping hours per month**.  
- Around **377 medically fit patients** are stuck in acute beds – effectively blocking an entire major hospital.  
- If we do nothing, these numbers will worsen, driving up costs, burnout, and avoidable harm.  
- Fixing flow is now a **fiscal, clinical, and ethical necessity** – not an optional reform.

### 2. The Solution – Flow First, Not Bricks First

This is **not** a 10-year construction plan. It is a **12-month system redesign** built around **13 integrated pillars**.

Core moves:

- **Accountability**  
  Appoint a single **Statewide Flow Controller** with clear authority to manage beds, transfers, and offloads across all LHNs, 24/7.

- **Capacity & Flow**
  - Stand up **Acute Medical Assessment Units (AMAUs)** and **Transit/Recovery Units** to create the missing “buffer” in the middle of the system.  
  - Launch a **Stranded Patient Taskforce** and **Bridging Fund** to rapidly move long-stay, medically fit patients into aged care, NDIS supports, or step-down beds.  
  - This frees up **100–200 acute bed equivalents** without building a single new hospital.

- **The AI Edge**  
  Invest **\$40–60M** in an AI platform that:
  - Forecasts **ramping and bed pressure 72 hours in advance**  
  - Optimises bed use, transfers, and staffing  
  - Automates low-value paperwork so staff can focus on patients and flow  

### 3. The Value – What We Get for the Money

#### 12-Month Targets

- **Monthly ramping hours**
  - Now: **5,550+ hours** (worst recent peak)  
  - Target: **< 3,000 hours** (a **40–50% reduction**)

- **Stranded acute patients**
  - Now: **~377 patients**  
  - Target: **< 200 patients**  
  - Effect: frees **100–200 acute beds** worth **\$40–90M per year** in avoided blocked-bed costs.

- **Cost of inaction**
  - Current waste: **\$40–90M per year** keeping medically fit patients in acute beds.  
  - The reform **recovers that money**, effectively paying for itself within **2–3 years**.

#### AI Return on Investment (10 Years)

- **AI layer cost:** \$40–60M build, then \$12–20M per year to run.  
- **Additional savings** from better prediction, optimisation, and admin automation:  
  - **Net benefit:** approximately **\$150–400M over 10 years**.

### 4. Financial & Political Strategy

- **Total reform envelope:** **\$450–600M** over 12 months.  
- **Net Year-1 cost after offsets:** approximately **\$350–400M**, declining as savings grow.

- **Federal co-funding**
  - Seek **\$160–240M** in Commonwealth support by framing this as a **National Flow Pilot**, directly tackling NDIS and aged-care gridlock.  
  - This **shares responsibility with Canberra** for fixing bottlenecks outside SA Health’s direct control.

- **Innovation & IP for South Australia**
  - Co-develop the AI platform with **SA universities and local industry**.  
  - SA retains key **intellectual property**, positioning the state as a national leader in digital health flow with technology that can be adapted and exported.

## South Australia Emergency Flow & Ramping Reform Plan

> **Title:** South Australia Emergency Flow & Ramping Reform Plan  
> **Subtitle:** “Fix the Flow, Not Just the Bricks”  
> **Version:** Draft v1.0 | 30 November 2025  

### Executive Summary

South Australia’s health system is experiencing sustained **access block**: ambulance ramping, ED overcrowding, and patients stuck in acute beds who no longer need to be there.

In the worst months:

- Ramping exceeded **5,500 hours per month**  
- Fewer than **half of ambulance patients** were offloaded within 30 minutes during peak winter surges  
- Around **377 medically fit patients** were occupying acute beds waiting for aged care or NDIS pathways to open  

This plan delivers a **12-month, flow-focused redesign** of the system – using the hospitals we already have more intelligently, rather than waiting a decade for new bricks.

#### The Problem – Three Doors Jammed

- **Front Door:** Too many low-acuity patients arrive by ambulance or walk into ED because they don’t know where else to go or can’t get timely GP/UCC care. During winter peaks, offload performance has fallen to **well under 50%** within 30 minutes at key metro sites, far below the **90% target**.  
- **Middle of the House:** Admitted ED patients wait too long for ward beds; short-stay assessment units and transit spaces are underdeveloped. This “hidden ramping” is the engine room of crowding.  
- **Back Door:** Hundreds of patients medically ready for discharge are stuck due to delays in aged care, NDIS, housing and rehab. This consumes the equivalent capacity of a **major metropolitan hospital**.

#### The Solution – 13 Pillars Built Around Flow, Not Bricks

The plan is built on three simple ideas:

1. Fix the flow at all three doors – **front, middle, and back**.  
2. Put **one person in charge** of the whole system’s flow – a **Statewide Flow Controller** with data and authority.  
3. Use **every bed in the state** – public and private – and every safe alternative to an acute bed.

**The 13 Pillars:**

1. Statewide Flow Controller & Data Spine  
2. 30-Minute Ambulance Offload Guarantee  
3. Front Door Diversions – GPs, UCCs, Virtual ED & public education  
4. Internal Buffer – AMAUs & Transit Units (plus MH crisis pods)  
5. Back Door – Discharge Blitz & 10am discharge standard  
6. Aged Care & NDIS Unblockers – Stranded Patient Taskforce & Bridging Fund  
7. Hospital-at-Home & Virtual Wards  
8. Smarter Ambulance Use & Chronic Caller Program  
9. Regional–Metro Transfers & Retrieval Service  
10. Workforce Burnout Circuit-Breaker  
11. Governance, KPIs & Public Dashboard (including equity metrics)  
12. Prediction & War-Gaming  
13. Communication & Change Management (including First Nations Flow Forum)

#### Cost – Headline Numbers

- **Gross 12-month program cost:** ≈ **\$450–\$600M**  
  - Planning midpoint ≈ **\$500M**  
  - Includes ~**10% contingency** for wage inflation and implementation risk  
- This is **under 6%** uplift on SA’s current health spend:  
  > “Half a billion to fix flow in 12 months vs billions for a single new hospital in the 2030s.”

#### Savings & Net Cost

By freeing **100–200 acute bed-equivalents** via better flow:

- Using **\$1,100 per acute bed-day**, the system saves:  
  - ≈ **\$40–90M per year** in avoided acute bed-days alone  

Additional gains:

- Reclaimed ambulance hours  
- Reduced overtime and agency spend  
- Improved use of private capacity  

**Net Year 1 cost after offsets:** ≈ **\$350–\$400M**  
**Year 2+:** trending towards **near-neutral**, as savings embed.

#### Timeline – When Does It Start to Bite?

- **Days 0–14:** Flow Centre live (basic dashboard), Flow Controller appointed, Taskforce & Bridging Fund activated, KPIs announced.  
- **Days 15–60:** First rapid offload / Transit Unit opens; ED–UCC hotline pilot; chronic caller program; first private contracts signed.  
- **Months 3–6:** AMAUs at major sites, Virtual ED for ambos, hospital-at-home scaled, public dashboard live.  
- **Months 6–12:** Full metro rollout, regional stabilisation & retrieval fully operational, prediction layer & war-gaming active.

> **Visible progress** within **90 days**; **structural improvement** within **12 months**.

#### Political Pitch

- **Problem:** “We inherited a system that was failing – 5,500 hours a month of ramping, hundreds of stranded patients, and fewer than half of arrivals offloaded within 30 minutes in winter peaks.”  
- **Solution:** “This is a 12-month flow redesign, not another 15-year building promise.”  
- **Value:** “We’re investing about half a billion dollars – under 6% of our health spend – to make the hospitals we already have actually work.”  
- **Equity:** “We’re fixing access block for everyone, with First Nations communities at the table from day one, not added on at the end.”

### Part 1 – Overview

#### 1.1 Objective

Reduce **ambulance ramping and ED overcrowding by 40–50% within 12 months** by redesigning how patients flow through South Australia’s health system – from first contact to safe discharge – focusing on **operational changes, governance, and equity**, not just capital projects.

#### 1.2 Core Idea – The Three Doors

The system fails at three points:

1. **Front Door (Inflow)** – who arrives, how, and where they go first.  
2. **Middle of the House (Internal Flow)** – how quickly admitted patients leave ED and move through short-stay and ward capacity.  
3. **Back Door (Outflow)** – how quickly medically fit patients leave acute beds for rehab, aged care, NDIS supports, or home.

The **13 pillars** map to these three doors plus cross-cutting governance and workforce supports.

#### 1.3 Design Principles

1. **Flow first, not bricks first** – use existing infrastructure smarter before building more.  
2. **Single point of accountability** – one Flow Controller with data and authority.  
3. **Short feedback loops** – rapid pilots, visible KPIs, quick iteration.  
4. **Equity embedded, not bolted on** – First Nations and vulnerable groups built into pathways and metrics.  
5. **Evidence-based, low-capital interventions** – AMAUs, transit units, hospital-at-home, retrieval services.  
6. **Transparency and consequences** – public dashboards and kill-switch thresholds so the plan cannot quietly fail.

#### 1.4 Hard Baseline – Where We’re Starting

Using worst recent data (e.g. July 2024 and winter peaks):

| Metric                    | Current Baseline (Worst)                          | Implication                                                        |
|---------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Monthly Ramping Hours     | **5,550+ hours/month**                            | >7 months of an ambulance “stuck” every month.                     |
| Ambulance Offload ≤30min  | **Well under 50%** in winter surges (target 90%) | Majority of arrivals fail the core performance standard in peaks. |
| Stranded Patients         | **~377 medically fit** patients in acute beds    | Equivalent to a major metropolitan hospital blocked.               |

#### 1.5 12-Month Targets & Success Metrics

| Metric                    | 12-Month Target                     | Required Change                  |
|---------------------------|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Monthly Ramping Hours     | **< 3,000 hours/month**             | **40–50% reduction**             |
| Offload ≤30 Minutes       | **≥ 75% of arrivals**               | ~30 percentage point improvement |
| Stranded Patients         | **< 200 patients**                  | 45%+ reduction                   |
| ED Admit-to-Ward Time     | 75% within 4 hours                  | Major internal flow improvement  |
| Indigenous Low-Acuity ED  | 10–15% reduction in target regions  | Equity & Closing the Gap impact  |

## AI Integration Plan for SA Health Flow & Ramping Reform

> **Title:** AI Integration Plan for SA Health Flow & Ramping Reform  
> **Version:** Draft v1.0 | 30 November 2025  

### 1. Purpose & Scope

AI will be integrated to:

- Improve **prediction and early warning** of access block  
- **Optimise** use of existing beds, staff and transport  
- **Automate** low-value administrative work  
- Protect **safety, equity and trust** (especially for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities)  
- Deliver a **net financial saving** over 10+ years  

AI is an **amplifier** for the 13 Pillars, not a replacement for clinical judgement or governance. All AI use is **Human-in-the-Loop**, auditable, and constrained by SA Health policy and law.

### 2. Core AI Capabilities

- **Prediction:** ramping, ED overcrowding, bed and staffing pressure, stranded risk.  
- **Optimisation:** bed allocation, transfer routing, staffing, discharge sequencing.  
- **Automation:** admin tasks, documentation, reporting.

### 3. High-Level Architecture

- **Statewide Data Spine** (extends Flow Centre): ED, bed, ambulance, aged care/NDIS, virtual care data.  
- **AI Services Layer:** forecasting, optimisation, navigation, document/workflow automation.  
- **Human-in-the-Loop Control:** AI suggests; humans decide. All with audit logs.  
- **Privacy, Security & Residency:** SA Health infrastructure, role-based access, de-identification, Indigenous data governance.

### 4. Priority AI Use-Cases Aligned to the Flow Reform

Key examples (full text above):

- **Ramping & access block forecasting** (24–72 hours).  
- **Hidden ramping metric** and Flow Centre alerts.  
- **Bed & transfer optimisation** across AMAUs, Transit Units, Retrieval Service.  
- **“Who Can Go Home?”** daily DRC-based discharge suggestion lists for MDT review.  
- **Aged care / NDIS matchmaking** via capacity register + Bridging Fund prompts.  
- **Virtual triage & navigation** for non-emergency demand.  
- **Ambulance Virtual ED support** for low-risk, well-defined cohorts.  
- **Rostering support** and **admin load reduction** (summaries, letters, coding).  
- **Equity metrics & Indigenous Cultural Flow Bridge** support.  
- **Continuous learning & pilot evaluation.**

### 5. Governance, Safety & Ethics for AI

- **Clinical approval** for each use-case; continuous monitoring.  
- **Indigenous data governance & equity assurance** (co-designed, sovereignty principles).  
- **Transparency & explainability** of recommendations; full override logs.  
- **Bias, fairness & audit** with thresholds for pulling or retraining models.  
- **Vendor & lock-in safeguards** via open standards, data ownership, and audit rights.

### 6. Delivery Roadmap (0–24 Months)

- **Phase 1 (0–6 months):** Foundations & quick wins (data spine extension, basic forecasting, pilots for “Who Can Go Home?” and simple automation).  
- **Phase 2 (6–12 months):** Integrated use-cases (scaling forecasting, discharge tools, virtual triage pilots, optimisation support).  
- **Phase 3 (12–24 months):** Optimisation & equity (full optimisation, roster tools, equity dashboards, BAU governance).

### 7. 10-Year Savings & ROI (AI Layer Only)

**Cost Profile**

- **Build (Years 0–2):** ≈ **\$40–60M**  
- **Ongoing operating (Year 3+):** ≈ **\$12–20M/year**

**Incremental Savings (steady state, vs non-AI baseline)**

- **Additional 30–70 bed-equivalents**  
  - ≈ **\$12–28M/year** saved  
- **Ramping & ambulance savings:** ≈ **\$1–4M/year**  
- **Admin & paperwork efficiency (100–200 FTE-equivalents):**  
  - ≈ **\$12–24M/year**, via redeployment to higher-value roles (no compulsory redundancies).

**Total Incremental AI Effect**

- **Gross annual savings:** ≈ **\$25–55M/year**  
- **AI operating cost:** ≈ **\$12–20M/year**  
- **Net annual savings:** ≈ **\$15–40M/year**

**10-Year Net Impact**

- **Gross savings:** ≈ **\$250–550M**  
- **Minus Opex (10 years):** ≈ **\$120–200M**  
- **Net 10-year savings:** ≈ **\$150–400M**

> **Framing line:**  
> A **\$40–60M AI upgrade** that pays for itself in **3–4 years**, then returns **\$150–400M** over a decade — while making the core ramping and flow reforms safer, fairer, and more effective.
